Privacy notice

This page explains how the staging app handles your data. It is not professional legal advice.

Your data

CSV files, JSON project files, colors, categories, and values are processed in your browser. The app has no backend, accounts, database, analytics, ads, or cloud project storage.

Browser storage

Autosave uses local storage in your browser. When the app upgrades an older autosave, it keeps the original compatibility copy until you explicitly delete it. Before a recovery, edit, project open, or Start over action replaces an unreadable current backup, the app keeps that current copy byte-for-byte in one local recovery slot.

Start over resets the workspace and removes the current autosave. Separate controls let you recover or delete the previous compatibility copy, download an unreadable current or compatibility copy as raw text, and download or delete a replaced browser backup. The app does not log or upload backup contents. All copies stay on this device and can be lost if you clear site data.

If you decline to open a valid saved copy at startup, that copy stays unchanged until the app can verify a safety copy before your first replacement edit.

Exports and downloads

SVG and PNG files, migration reports, and data-report templates are created in your browser. You decide which files to download or share.

Network requests

During normal startup, the app loads static files from the same staging domain. These files include HTML, CSS, JavaScript, local Leaflet files, and the standard map data. Higher-detail boundary data is loaded only when you choose a detailed export.

Hosting logs

Cloudflare may process standard technical logs to provide hosting. The app does not add third-party tracking.

Contact

No public contact channel has been verified in the repository. Use the data-report template to prepare a report. You can send it manually after the owner provides a public contact destination.
