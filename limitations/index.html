Data and app limitations

NusaCanvas is designed for presentation maps and lightweight reports. It is not a legal record, an official mapping system, or a claim about the latest administrative boundaries.

2020 boundary snapshot

The map uses 2020 second-level administrative boundaries (ADM2) from geoBoundaries/HDX. Current information about Indonesia's provinces is kept in a separate 2025 region list.

519 mapped shapes

The number 519 is the shape count in the boundary file. It is not a claim about the current number of autonomous regencies and cities.

53 records need more evidence

Region names are not assigned official information when the match is uncertain. Their shapes can still be colored, but their official details need review.

Visual exports

SVG and PNG exports are for visual use, not precise measurement or legal boundary decisions. The current PDF is a raster image, so its text cannot be selected.

Performance limits

The app starts with simplified boundaries to stay responsive. A detailed export loads a local file of about 10.5 MB only after you choose it and may use more memory.

Spreadsheet limits

NusaCanvas does not silently combine several source rows into one region. You must resolve or ignore duplicate rows. XLSX safety checks limit browser processing but do not scan files for malware. Very large projects can exceed browser storage.

Legal and license limits

Keep all required data-source attribution. The staging privacy notice and terms are operational drafts and do not replace professional legal advice.
